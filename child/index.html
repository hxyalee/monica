Africa is Falling Behind

Child mortality rates remain drastically higher then the global average

We are using child moratlity rates as a key indicator to understand healthcare standards because it reflects the access to basic health interventions such as vaccinations, medical treatments and nutrition.

As the map here shows, today the highest child mortality rates are in Sub-Saharan Africa, where we still have countries with child mortality rates greater than 10%.

Despite this considerable progress, improving child survival remains a matter of urgent concern. In 2019 alone, roughly 14,000 under-five deaths occurred every day, an intolerably high number of largely preventable child deaths.
